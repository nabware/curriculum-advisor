Capstone Project Requirement
• Application track: The project should combine one or more GenAI
  models (text, image, audio, video) with an interactive application
  (chatbot, web app, or tool).
• Research track (up to 20 extra credit): find one topic in class, read
  related papers, design frameworks/methods or replicate SOTA
  methods on new models, do experiments, analyze, and write a paper at
  the end. (CSC 803 students are highly recommended to choose this
  one.)
• It should be achievable in ~7 weeks of team effort.
   • CSC 803 students: needs some level of innovation in the project.
• Deliverables:
   • CSC 603 Students: Final presentation (required). A publishable paper (one
     paper per team) may be submitted for extra credit (up to 10 points per student,
     depending on the quality and effort).
   • CSC 803 Students: Final presentation (required) and a report or paper
     (required).
Application track Example Projects/Ideas
Text-based GenAI
AI Study Assistant – A chatbot that answers course-specific questions using retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).
AI Grader – Automatically grades short-answer or coding assignments with rubrics and provides feedback.
Curriculum Advisor – Helps students choose courses based on prerequisites, interests, and career goals.
Multimodal / Media Generation
AI-Powered Presentation Maker – e.g., Generate slides from research papers.
Story-to-Comic Generator – Converts a short story into illustrated panels with text + images.
Podcast Assistant – Transcribes, summarizes, and generates show notes from audio.
Productivity & Creativity
AI Travel Agent – Builds itineraries, books activities, and answers travel-related queries.
Meeting Summarizer – Tool that transcribes and produces concise summaries with action items.
Personalized Study Planner – Generates tailored study schedules with motivational nudges.
Specialized Applications
Secure Code Reviewer – An AI assistant that reviews code for vulnerabilities (SQL injection, XSS, buffer overflow) and
suggests fixes.
AI Medical Support – A “doctor chatbot” for common minor illnesses (cold, flu, allergies) that provides guidance and
disclaimers.
Research Track Project Steps
1. Find research topic: Refer to the topics in slides, pick one that
   interests you, read the papers listed on the relevant slides
2. Do literature scanning: go to https://scholar.google.com/ or
   https://arxiv.org/ to find State-Of-The-Art (SOTA) research in this
   area.
3. Identify gaps (what you can do to improve?) and come up with a
   research question
4. Design and conduct experiment
5. Analyze experiment results and write the paper
