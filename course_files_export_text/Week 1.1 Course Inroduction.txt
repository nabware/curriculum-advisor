Course Focus
• Target Audience: already tried out ChatGPT or other generative
  AI tools, and curious about what's actually going on behind the
  scenes
    • GenAI is changing fast, we'll focus more on the concepts and
      principles behind it.
Why is it important to know the principle?
Generative AI is like magic. This course reveals the
techniques behind the magic so you can truly
understand how it works.

                      Topics and Schedule
1. Introduction to Generative AI
   a. Overview: Definition and key characteristics of Generative AI. (w1)
   b. Prompt Engineering: understand how GenAI model work and know how to
      interreact with them effectively (W2)
   c. AI Agent (W3&4)
   d. How a model is trained (W5&6)
   e. Post-training and forgetting (W7)
   f. Inside Large Language Model (LLM) and its evolutions. (W8&11, no class on
      W9&10)
   g. How to evaluate the performance of an LLM (W12)
   h. Ethics and security issues (W12)
   i. Image, audio, and video Generative AI Models (W13&14)
2. GenAI in Software Development: AI-driven tools for
   developers. (W15)
3. Team-based Capstone Project (Building GenAI-powered applications
   with open-source models, APIs, frameworks, etc.): Final presentation
   and Q&A. (W16)
Capstone Project
• Team
  • 3 students/team
  • 1 team leader (TL, responsibility: project management, task allocation &
    coordination, communication & meetings, etc.) – up to 10 extra credits
  • Form your own team. fill out a form (will be published on Canvas) by the 4th
    week (2/20), Students who do not form a team on their own by the deadline
    will be manually assigned to a team from the remaining students.
• Timeline
  • W1-5 form teams by 2/20 (Friday)
  • W8 TL submit proposal (I will provide template on W5) due W8 before class
  • W11 checkpoint: TL submit progress report, due W11 before class
  • W16 (second week of Dec.) presentation and peer evaluation
